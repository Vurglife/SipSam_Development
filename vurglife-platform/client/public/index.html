Game Overview

SipSam is a Caribbean card game played between 4 players — one designated Banker and three regular players. Each player receives 13 cards from a standard 52-card deck. Players compete solely against the Banker — never each other. The Banker role is randomly assigned at game start and remains fixed for that game.

Table Selection & Bank Requirements

TableMin BetIncrementMax BetMin BankWallet

Your bank must meet the minimum to enter. Your wallet is funded from your bank on entry and returned on exit.

Dealing Logic

One card is flipped face-up. Its value determines who receives the first card dealt:

  • A, 5, 9, K → Banker receives first
  • 2, 6, 10 → Player 1
  • 3, 7, J → Player 2
  • 4, 8, Q → Player 3

Cards then rotate clockwise until each player holds 13 cards.

Hand Arrangement (3 Hands)

Each player splits 13 cards into 3 hands arranged from weakest → strongest toward themselves:

  • 1st Hand — 3 cards — weakest — placed furthest from player (toward center)
  • 2nd Hand — 5 cards — stronger than 1st — placed in middle
  • 3rd Hand — 5 cards — strongest — placed closest to player

⚠️ Hands MUST be in ascending strength order: 1st (weakest) → 2nd → 3rd (strongest). Violation = disqualification.

Betting

Bets are placed each round before declaring. Minimum is the table default; maximum is the table limit. Bets cannot be reduced below the table minimum.

  • $100 Table: $100 → $150 in $50 increments
  • $250 Table: $250 → $500 in $50 increments
  • $500 Table: $500 → $1,000 in $100 increments
  • $1,000 Table: $1,000 → $2,000 in $500 increments

Hand Values (Strongest → Weakest)

  1. Royal Flush — A K Q J 10 same suit
  2. Straight Flush — 5 consecutive same suit
  3. Four of a Kind
  4. Full House — 3-of-a-kind + pair
  5. Flush — 5 same suit (beats Straight in SipSam)
  6. Straight — 5 consecutive mixed
  7. Three of a Kind
  8. Two Pair
  9. One Pair
  10. High Card

🃏 Flush ALWAYS beats Straight in SipSam — this differs from standard poker.

Tie-Breaking

  • Banker wins all ties — except flush ties
  • Flush ties broken by suit: Hearts > Spades > Diamonds > Clubs
  • Within same suit flush: compare cards positionally, highest card first

Declaration Timer

After dealing, each player has a limited time to arrange their hands and declare. Failure to declare within the timer = automatic disqualification for that round.

Send & Receive Chips (In-Game)

  • Request Chips — send a request message to a specific player or the Banker
  • Chip transfers are instantly deducted from sender's wallet

Replenishing Chips Mid-Game

If your wallet runs low, use Replenish Wallet from the in-game menu. Chips are drawn from your bank balance, limited to the table's wallet size. Your bank must have sufficient funds.

Game Overview

Rhum32 is a Caribbean casino card game played with 1 standard deck. Up to 6 players compete against the Dealer (House). The goal is to have a hand value lower than the Dealer's. If the Dealer's hand crosses 32, the Dealer automatically loses.

Card Values

  • Ace = 1
  • 2–9 = face value
  • 10, Jack, Queen, King = 10 each

Zero Rules (Cards Worth 0)

Certain card combinations within your hand count as 0:

  • 3 of a Kind — three cards of the same rank = 0
  • 4 of a Kind — four cards of the same rank = 0
  • Straight Flush (3+ cards) — consecutive cards of the same suit = 0

Example: 5♣ 6♣ 7♣ 3♥ 6♥ = hand value 9 (the 5,6,7 of clubs = 0, so 3+6 = 9)

Game Flow

  1. Front Bet — Players place their Front Bet (10 seconds). Optional Tie Bet also placed now.
  2. Deal 4 Cards — Dealer deals 4 cards to each player and themselves. Dealer's 4th card is shown face-up.
  3. Decision (10s) — Players see their 4 cards + dealer's shown card. Choose:
    • Bet (Stay In) — Pay a Back Bet = 2x Front Bet
    • Push (Fold) — Surrender Front Bet, sit out the round
  4. Deal 5th Card — Remaining players and dealer receive their final card.
  5. Reveal & Payout — All hands shown, payouts calculated.

Tie Bet

Optional bet placed before the round. If your hand value equals the Dealer's, the Tie Bet pays 20:1. Your Front and Back Bets are returned on a tie. If you don't tie, the Tie Bet is lost.

Dealer Crosses 32

If the Dealer's 5-card hand exceeds 32, all remaining players win their Front Bet 1:1. Back Bets are returned. No special multipliers apply on a bust.

Freeze Bets

Players can freeze their bet amount so it carries over automatically between rounds. Unfreeze to change bets during the next betting window.

Texas Hold'em

Overview: Texas Hold'em is the world's most popular poker variant. Each player receives 2 hole cards, then 5 community cards are dealt face-up. Build the best 5-card hand from any combination of your hole cards and community cards.

Betting Rounds: Pre-Flop (after hole cards), Flop (3 community cards), Turn (4th card), River (5th card). Actions: Check, Bet, Call, Raise, Fold, All-In.

Hand Rankings (high to low): Royal Flush, Straight Flush, Four of a Kind, Full House, Flush, Straight, Three of a Kind, Two Pair, One Pair, High Card.

Blinds: Small blind (half the min bet) and big blind (min bet) rotate each hand. The player left of the dealer posts the small blind.

Black Jack

Overview: Beat the dealer by getting a hand value closer to 21 without going over. Number cards are face value, face cards are 10, Aces are 1 or 11.

Actions: Hit (draw another card), Stand (keep your hand), Double Down (double your bet and draw exactly one more card), Split (if you have a pair, split into two hands).

Blackjack: An Ace + 10-value card on the initial deal is a Blackjack and pays 3:2. Dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more.

Insurance: When the dealer shows an Ace, you can take insurance (side bet at 2:1) against the dealer having Blackjack.
